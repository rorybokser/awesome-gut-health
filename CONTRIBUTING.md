# Contributing

Thanks for helping make this list better. A few guidelines keep it useful and trustworthy.

## What belongs here

- Apps, tools, food databases, diets, recipes, research, books, podcasts, and communities that are **genuinely useful** for managing IBD, IBS, or elimination diets.
- Resources that are **actively maintained** and from a **credible** source (clinicians, established orgs, peer-reviewed research, or well-regarded community projects).

## What doesn't

- Affiliate links, paid placements, or "submit your product for a fee" entries.
- Dead, abandoned, or thin resources.
- Unverified health claims or "miracle cure" framing.
- Anything that requires payment to access its core value, unless it's clearly the best in its category and labeled as paid.

## Format

- Add entries in the existing format: `- [Name](https://url) - One neutral, factual sentence.`
- Keep descriptions free of marketing language and superlatives.
- Place new entries in the most relevant section, alphabetically where the section is alphabetical.
- Use `https` links and check that they resolve.

## How to submit

Open a pull request with your addition. Briefly explain in the PR why the resource is useful and credible. Maintainers may edit descriptions for consistency.

By contributing, you agree to release your contributions under the CC0 license.
